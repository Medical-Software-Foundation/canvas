# HCC003v1 - Diabetes Mellitus With Secondary Complication Suspect

### Target Population
All patients with an active "Diabetes without complications" (ICD-10 E11.9) diagnosis on file.

### Recommendations
For any patient in the target population, the protocol checks for other active conditions that are commonly caused by diabetes. When one or more of these is present, the protocol surfaces a "Diagnose" recommendation on a protocol card for each matching category, suggesting the diagnosis be updated to a more specific code:

- Suspect eye condition - update to "Diabetes with secondary eye disease"
- Suspect neurologic condition - update to "Diabetes with secondary neurological sequela"
- Suspect renal condition - update to "Diabetes with secondary renal disease"
- Suspect circulatory condition - update to "Diabetes with secondary circulatory disorder"
- Other suspect complication - update to "Diabetes with other secondary complication"

When the patient has uncomplicated diabetes but no associated secondary complication is present, the protocol card is marked satisfied. Patients without an active uncomplicated-diabetes diagnosis are not in the denominator and the protocol emits no effects.

### Importance
Capturing the most specific diabetes diagnosis improves clinical documentation, supports accurate problem-list hygiene, and ensures the patient's risk picture is fully represented for downstream care coordination and risk adjustment.
